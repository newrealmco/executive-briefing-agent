## Daily briefing structure

Subject: Briefing — {date} | {top_theme}

---

### Today's focus
[One paragraph. The most important framing for the day — what the dominant signal is
and why it matters right now.]

---

### Read now ({N} items)

For each item:
**[Title]** — {source}
Why it matters: [one sentence, specific to your context]
TL;DR: [2–3 sentences]
Action: [Read now / Save for later / Reply / Monitor / Ignore]
Link: {url}

---

### Needs action
- [Email or item requiring a response, deadline, or follow-up]
- [Opportunity or signal requiring action within 48 hours]

---

### One recommended move
[Single concrete action for today — specific, not generic.]

---

## Weekly briefing structure

Subject: Weekly briefing — week of {date}

---

### What changed this week
[2–3 paragraph overview of the most significant movements across all topics.]

---

### Main themes
[3–5 recurring signals across sources, with brief explanation of why each theme matters.]

---

### Worth deeper reading
[3–5 items with title, source, why it matters, and link.]

---

### Why this matters specifically
[Connects the week's signals to your current projects, career positioning,
and any open decisions.]

---

### Recommended actions for next week
[3–5 concrete actions. Specific. Not generic.]

---

### What you may be missing
[Topics or signals you have not engaged with that deserve attention.
Recurring deferrals. Blind spots.]
